# Event Art Story Briefs

This document expands every Spirebound event into a small story and an artwork
brief. Use it before generating, editing, or reviewing `src/assets/events/*.png`.

The event image should show the moment of decision, not the result. It should
make the written event feel inevitable without revealing which branch the player
will choose.

Production event art uses a two-step raster pass:

```text
built-in GPT image source -> Nano Banana Pro full-scene clean-up -> gallery registration
```

All events must pass through Nano Banana Pro before they are considered for
gallery registration. For event art, do not request alpha, chroma-key, cutout,
or transparent-background control unless the workflow is revised; the background
is part of the story.

## Story-To-Art Rules

- Start with what happened before the player arrived.
- Freeze the scene at the choice moment.
- Put the decision object or figure in the foreground.
- Use one midground prop as evidence of the story.
- Use two or three broad background shapes to identify the place.
- Keep the background lower contrast and lower line count than the subject.
- Never show a random branch result as already true.
- Never add UI, text, labels, card frames, choice buttons, or reward callouts.

## Event Briefs

### `forgottenShrine` - Forgotten Shrine

Game context: remove a card, desecrate for gold plus Hex, or leave.

Small story: Climbers once fed this shrine with silver, bone, and unwanted
memories. The stone learned the shape of their bargains. When the player
arrives, moss has sealed most offerings away, but a fresh glimmer still hums at
the base and something inside the shrine is awake.

Decision moment: the player is close enough to pray or steal, but the shrine has
not yet answered.

Artwork design: foreground moss-eaten shrine with a single eye-like stone seam;
midground bone and silver offerings plus one cracked card shard half-buried in
moss; background broad root arches and dim chapel stones. Use jade moss,
tarnished silver, old bone, amber shrine warmth, and black lead.

Avoid: a guaranteed gold pile, a visible monster, tiny runes, dense vines, or a
full temple interior.

### `woundedKnight` - The Wounded Knight

Game context: lose HP to aid him for a relic, loot him for gold plus Hex, or
leave.

Small story: A knight dragged a relic out of a broken chapel battle and made it
as far as one shattered pillar. He is still alive, but only barely. His offered
hand asks for mercy; his fallen purse and broken blade tempt the player to take
what remains.

Decision moment: the relic is extended, the knight is breathing, and the player
has not yet chosen aid or theft.

Artwork design: foreground crushed stained-glass knight holding a warm relic;
midground broken pillar, fallen banner, and a small coin pouch near the gauntlet;
background broad chapel arches and cold blue glass light. Use luminous bruised
blue, pale silver, amber relic light, muted red wound glass, and black lead.

Avoid: heroic standing posture, excessive armour segments, gore, extra soldiers,
or making the relic look already collected.

### `voidChest` - Humming Chest

Game context: open a 55 percent relic / 45 percent damage gamble, or leave.

Small story: This treasury alcove was sealed from the outside, not the inside.
The lock has already broken and the chest keeps singing the note that ruined the
last hand that touched it. It may contain a relic, or it may only be bait.

Decision moment: the lid is still shut, but the seam hints at teeth.

Artwork design: foreground chunky black iron chest with violet glass panels,
broken amber lock, and a mouth-like lid seam; midground broken key and cracked
stone step; background broad treasury arches and coloured light pools. Use black
iron, violet, cold blue, amber lock glow, and a tiny sickly green danger accent.

Avoid: showing the relic clearly, showing the damage outcome, floor-grid detail,
or a monster emerging from the chest.

### `emberFountain` - Fountain of Embers

Game context: heal, bottle a healing potion, or leave.

Small story: A battle was won here long ago, and the fountain kept the warmth of
that victory after the names were forgotten. Its embers are liquid rather than
flame. They mend glass, but only while the basin still remembers kindness.

Decision moment: sparks rise without burning, inviting either a bath or a
careful bottle.

Artwork design: foreground cracked basin filled with liquid ember light;
midground small empty phial and worn kneeling stone; background broad courtyard
arches and soft summer-gold haze. Use ember orange, dawn pink, warm gold, pale
stone, and black lead.

Avoid: lava danger, violent fire, skull motifs, guaranteed potion emphasis, or a
busy garden.

### `forge` - The Forgotten Forge

Game context: upgrade a card, or leave.

Small story: The forge outlived its smiths. Its hammers still hang in order and
the anvil still holds heat, waiting for a hand brave enough to place a card in
the old work-light.

Decision moment: the hot card pane rests on the anvil and the hammer has not yet
fallen.

Artwork design: foreground black star-metal anvil with a hot card-shaped glass
pane; midground oversized hammer and furnace mouth; background broad forge
arches and amber-blue heat planes. Use black star-metal, ember orange, deep
cobalt, tarnished gold, pale furnace glow, and black lead.

Avoid: many chains, busy machinery, sparks covering the silhouette, or making it
look like a shop.

### `gambler` - The Bone Gambler

Game context: bet 40 gold for a 45 percent chance to win 110 gold, or walk away.

Small story: The gambler has played this table through several disasters and
keeps the losing bones. He offers one throw because one throw is enough. The cup
rattles with old knuckles, and the table has marks where desperate hands once
pressed too hard.

Decision moment: the cup is tilted, the bones are not yet cast, and the wager is
still voluntary.

Artwork design: foreground grinning figure with a bone cup and one open palm;
midground simple ritual table with three chunky bone dice and a small coin
stack; background broad tavern-crypt arch shapes and low amber light. Use bone
white, old gold, smoky violet, warm amber, and black lead.

Avoid: visible text on dice, showing the winning or losing throw, piles of money
as guaranteed reward, or a crowd of gamblers.

### `mirror` - The Silvered Mirror

Game context: duplicate a card, shatter it to lose HP and remove a card, or
leave.

Small story: The mirror survived the collapse because it wanted to. Every person
who stood before it left a little delay behind. Now the player's reflection
moves late and smiles early, offering either a copy or a clean break.

Decision moment: the reflection is half a breath out of sync, and the mirror is
still intact.

Artwork design: foreground tall silver mirror with a slightly delayed silhouette
inside; midground one sharp stone and a reflected card pane; background broad
rubble arches and cold moonlit glass. Use silver blue, pale cyan, violet shadow,
small amber edge light, and black lead.

Avoid: a detailed player portrait, horror-face excess, shattered outcome already
happening, or tiny mirror filigree.

### `library` - The Drowned Library

Game context: choose a card from five, or heal among the stacks.

Small story: The library drowned slowly enough that some books learned to glow
underwater. Most shelves are pulp, but a few pages still rise like fish around a
dry ledge where a traveller could rest.

Decision moment: the player has found the glowing pages and the quiet resting
place in the same flooded aisle.

Artwork design: foreground glowing pages floating above shallow water; midground
waterlogged shelf and a dry stack of books like a small bed; background broad
submerged arches and blue-green light planes. Use deep teal, pale page gold,
water blue, soft green, and black lead.

Avoid: too many readable books, text on pages, a monster in the water, or a deep
landscape.

### `fleshTrader` - The Flesh Trader

Game context: lose Max HP for a relic, or refuse.

Small story: The trader wears a merchant's coat because customers trust coats
more than faces. He does not ask for gold. His scales measure warmth, breath,
and the future years inside the body. The relic in his hand is real, which makes
the offer worse.

Decision moment: the relic is visible in one hand while the other hand measures
the player's life without touching it.

Artwork design: foreground long-fingered merchant-coat figure offering a relic;
midground small balance scale with a red glass heart-weight; background broad
market-tent or alley arches fading into magenta shadow. Use bruised magenta,
merchant black, warm relic gold, pale flesh-pink glass used sparingly, and black
lead.

Avoid: gore, exposed organs, extra customers, text price tags, or making the
figure look like a normal shopkeeper.

### `cursedIdol` - The Cursed Idol

Game context: take a relic plus Hex, or leave it.

Small story: The idol is valuable because everyone who understood it is already
gone. It radiates comfort like a trap with manners. The skull plinth is not a
threat; it is a receipt.

Decision moment: the idol's warmth reaches the player, but it has not yet been
lifted from the plinth.

Artwork design: foreground jade idol with a soft dangerous glow; midground skull
plinth and one abandoned relic chain; background broad jungle-shrine stones and
dark green light planes. Use jade, sickly green, old bone, warm gold, and black
lead.

Avoid: showing the Hex card as a UI object, too many skulls, a living cultist,
or making it look purely benevolent.

### `ruinedCamp` - Ruined Camp

Game context: rest for healing, or scavenge gold plus a random potion.

Small story: Whoever made this camp left in the middle of a breath. The fire is
still alive, the bedrolls are torn, and a pack lies open with useful things
inside. The danger has moved on, but the silence has not.

Decision moment: the player can sit by the fire or search the abandoned packs.

Artwork design: foreground smouldering campfire and torn bedroll; midground open
pack with a small phial glint and a few coins; background broad tree or ruin
silhouettes with dawn-grey light. Use warm campfire amber, muted canvas brown,
cool blue-grey, small phial colour accent, and black lead.

Avoid: dead bodies, active attackers, too much loot, cosy tavern mood, or a busy
forest scene.

## Production Prompt Pattern

For each event, convert the brief into one image prompt using this order:

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: Spirebound event art full-background source
Primary request: <event name>, <decision moment from this document>.
Small story: <two sentence story capsule from this document>.
Narrative read: show <foreground> first, <midground choice prop> second, <background place cue> third.
Style/medium: serious cartoon-gothic stained-glass game art; chunky black outer contour on the main subject; 3-5 large jewel-tone colour masses; very few thick lead dividers; matte painterly glass texture; warm amber rim light; controlled inner glow.
Composition/framing: wide 3:2 event scene; centre-right focal; foreground/midground/background depth; generous breathing room; no hard card frame; no cropped focal prop.
Lighting/mood: luminous midtones; background lower contrast than subject; gothic decision mood without crushing darkness.
Palette: <event palette>.
Constraints: no text; no labels; no watermark; no buttons; no UI chrome; no full card mockup; no guaranteed random outcome; avoid detailed lattice, floor grids, tiny filigree, and busy background architecture.
```

Then run Nano Banana Pro with the GPT source as an input reference:

```text
帮我将这张图片重绘和清晰化，让它细节更丰富，同时去掉原图中杂乱不必要的细节。重要：这次不做 alpha、chroma-key、cutout 或透明背景控制；请保留完整的 3:2 Spirebound event scene 和原图的叙事背景。保留 <foreground subject> 作为清晰前景主体；保留 <midground choice prop>；背景保留 <broad place cues>. 整体要像严肃 cartoon-gothic stained-glass game art：主主体有更粗的黑色外轮廓、更大的形块、更少碎裂玻璃、更少地面碎片，128px 仍然能读。背景可以有深度和叙事，但对比度和线条数量必须低于主体。保留 <event palette>. 不要加入人物、文字、UI、明显奖励堆、额外供品、玻璃碎片、地板图案、符文、复杂建筑或阻挡主体的前景物。
```
