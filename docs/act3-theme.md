# Act 3 Theme — The Cracked Astral Court

Act 3 is not generic void, space, or purple magic. It is the broken astral court
at the top of the Obsidian Spire: a ruined royal observatory of judgement eyes,
crown shards, orbit rings, star-fire, black stone, and theatrical end-times
ceremony.

The tone is final-act serious, but the enemy designs must keep the approved
readable enemy personality: funny but threatening, odd in proportion, and built
around one instantly readable silhouette idea.

## Shared Characteristic

Every Act 3 enemy should carry the same faction read:

**Broken orbit halo plus magenta judgement core.**

This is not a stamped badge. It should mutate per character so it supports the
silhouette:

- a broken orbit ring, halo fragment, collar, eye frame, star chart, horn rim,
  chest seal, crown-back, or cracked court device
- one hot magenta judgement core, used as the clearest glow focal point
- tiny tarnished-gold court accents, used sparingly

The orbit fragment must help the character read at 128px. If it becomes small
ornament, replace it with a larger silhouette feature.

## Palette

Use a limited Act 3 palette:

- obsidian black for lead lines, silhouette weight, and cracks
- bright bruised violet or electric amethyst glass
- hot magenta judgement glow
- cold cyan-white or blue-white star glass
- tiny tarnished-gold court accents

Do not make every character a purple-black humanoid. The shared motif should
bind the set; each enemy still needs its own body shape and comic flaw. Because
the runtime combat and gallery backgrounds are dark, avoid large unlit black
body panels. The black should frame brighter glass masses, not swallow them.

## Prompt Rules

Keep the successful first-three-enemies prompt structure:

- short subject description before extra rules
- one iconic primary read
- explicit left-facing enemy pose
- simple body-shape language
- limited palette
- funny but threatening personality
- no heavy ban list beyond the baseline noise controls
- `#00ff00` chroma-key backdrop for enemies with a hot magenta judgement core
- dark-background readability: large colour masses must be sharp enough to read
  on near-black UI backgrounds

For Act 3, add the shared motif after the primary read:

```text
Act 3 faction read: include one broken orbit halo fragment and one hot magenta
judgement core, adapted to this creature's silhouette; add only tiny
tarnished-gold court accents.
```

Avoid:

- generic galaxy or nebula body
- elegant space wizard
- noble royal armour as the main read
- repeated clean upright humanoid stance
- overusing crowns until every enemy feels like a boss
- tiny faction marks that disappear at 128px
- ornate astrolabe lacework or micro star panels

## Enemy Directions

Use these as subject notes, not rigid prompts.

- `voidWisp`: approved v2 colour-lift direction; a tiny runaway court candle or
  star-flame ghost, with a cracked orbit ring as a little broken candlestick and
  a magenta core in the belly. Keep the sharper electric violet, sapphire,
  cyan-white, and hot magenta glass read from the approved candidate.
- `obsidianGolem`: approved v2 colour-lift direction; a squat palace brick with
  one cracked star-core, a broken orbit ring stuck through one shoulder like a
  ruined arch. Keep enough bright violet/cyan/magenta glass so the obsidian body
  does not disappear on dark backgrounds.
- `starCultist`: approved v2 colour-lift direction; a ridiculous little
  astrologer clutching an oversized star chart or orbit disc, more nervous court
  clerk than elegant priest. Keep the bright orbit chart as the primary read.
- `shade`: approved v1 colour-lift direction; a tattered court cloak with
  almost no body, a large broken crescent orbit clasp, and a sneaky magenta face
  void. Keep the enemy read as an empty cloak rather than a robed hero.
- `chaosHound`: approved v1 colour-lift direction; a broken comet dog with a
  jagged orbit collar, tiny mad eyes, and a magenta chest spark; feral, not
  noble. Keep the bright comet head and oversized tail as the funny threat read.
- `watcherEye`: approved v1 colour-lift direction; a pompous floating judgement
  eye with the orbit ring as its eyelid frame, annoyed and over-official. Keep
  the giant eye as the single primary shape even when court tassels are present.
- `voidColossus`: an oversized royal statue or court pillar that is too heavy
  to move properly, with a huge cracked orbit seal in its chest. The approved
  v2 direction stays cleaner than v1: broad pillar panes, one shoulder orbit
  arch, no chains, no brick-mosaic body texture.
- `heraldOfEnd`: a melodramatic doomsday announcer with a giant horn, banner,
  or bell-mouth framed by a broken orbit ring. The approved v2 direction uses
  the horn as the primary silhouette and avoids dangling ribbons, tassels, and
  tiny hanging tags.
- `sovereign`: the boss as cracked crown, scepter, black-sun halo, and magenta
  judgement core; terrifying and final, but still top-heavy and readable. The
  approved v2 direction is a clean astral-court totem: crown, black-sun halo,
  chest core, and scepter only, with no spiky crown forest or pendant noise.

## Act Separation

Act 1 already owns ash woods, roots, beasts, mushrooms, sap hearts, and grave
stone.

Act 2 already owns drowned city, shells, brine, eels, lures, sea-cathedrals, and
deep mouths.

Act 3 should own court judgement, broken orbit geometry, star-fire, obsidian
slabs, watching eyes, crown fragments, and final ceremony.
